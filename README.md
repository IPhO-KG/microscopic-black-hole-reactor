# Stability of a Microscopic Black Hole Reactor

Analytical and numerical investigation of whether Hawking evaporation
can be balanced by Bondi accretion to stabilise a microscopic black hole
as an energy source.

## Main Question

Can a microscopic black hole be maintained at a stable mass by feeding
it matter from a surrounding medium?

The mass dynamics are modelled by

\[
\dot{M}=AM^2-\frac{B}{M^2},
\]

where the first term represents Bondi accretion and the second represents
Hawking evaporation.

The model admits a critical mass

\[
M_{\mathrm{crit}}=\left(\frac{B}{A}\right)^{1/4},
\]

but this equilibrium is unstable.

## Main Results

For a microscopic black hole with initial mass

\[
M_0=10^7\ \mathrm{kg},
\]

immersed in water, the model predicts:

- evaporation lifetime of approximately 23.6 hours;
- critical mass of approximately \(3.12\times10^{10}\) kg;
- Hawking evaporation exceeding Bondi accretion by approximately
  fourteen orders of magnitude at the reference mass;
- an unstable critical point separating evaporation and accretion regimes.

Within the assumptions of this model, passive stabilisation through
Bondi accretion is not physically viable.

## Methods

The project includes:

- Hawking temperature and radiation-power derivations;
- evaporation lifetime calculation;
- approximate Bondi accretion modelling;
- nonlinear differential-equation analysis;
- fixed-point stability analysis;
- numerical integration of mass trajectories;
- parameter-space analysis for different surrounding media.

## Limitations

The model assumes spherical symmetry, a uniform surrounding medium,
semiclassical Hawking radiation, and approximate Bondi accretion.

Therefore, the conclusion applies to the assumptions of the present
model rather than to every hypothetical black-hole engineering system.
